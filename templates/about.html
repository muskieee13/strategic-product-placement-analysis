About This Project

Investigating the relationship between product positioning, sales performance, and consumer behavior

Project Objective

This project investigates the relationship between product positioning, sales performance, and consumer behavior. Using Tableau, we analyze retail sales data to uncover insights into how different positioning strategies impact sales and consumer preferences. By visualizing the data, we provide actionable recommendations to optimize product positioning strategies and drive revenue growth.

A retail company wants to understand the impact of product positioning on its sales and consumer behavior. They have collected data on sales figures, product placement, and consumer demographics. Through data visualization with Tableau, the company gains actionable insights to improve product positioning strategies and increase revenue.

Business Scenarios

Film & Television Production

Production companies can optimize revenue generation through brand partnerships. By employing Tableau visualization, they can analyze the effectiveness of product placements in different scenes or episodes, negotiate better deals with brands, understand audience engagement with specific products, and make data-driven decisions on future placement opportunities.

Retail & Consumer Goods

Retailers and consumer goods companies can enhance their marketing strategies and boost sales. Using Tableau, they track product performance across store locations and websites, identify high-traffic areas, understand customer preferences, and optimize product placement to increase visibility and drive conversions.

Advertising Agencies

Advertising agencies can provide valuable insights to their clients and optimize advertising campaigns. By utilizing Tableau, they analyze the impact of product placements across different media channels (movies, TV shows, online videos), demonstrate ROI of placement initiatives, refine targeting strategies, and improve campaign effectiveness.

Key Insights & Recommendations
Insights
  • Electronics generates the highest revenue per transaction due to premium pricing.
  • Clothing has the highest transaction volume — the most popular product category.
  • The 26–45 age group is the primary revenue driver across all categories.
  • Spending patterns across genders are relatively balanced with minor category preferences.
  • Seasonal trends show peaks during holiday months (Nov–Dec) and mid-year (May–Jun).
Recommendations
  • Premium Placement for Electronics: Prioritize high-visibility placements for electronics to maximize revenue.
  • Volume Strategy for Clothing: Increase shelf space and online presence for clothing items.
  • Targeted Marketing: Focus campaigns on the 26–45 demographic for maximum ROI.
  • Seasonal Campaigns: Align promotional activities with identified peak sales periods.
  • Cross-Category Bundling: Create product bundles combining high-volume and high-value categories.
